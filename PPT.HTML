3 / 8 • Causes

Why Punjab floods

🌧 Heavy Monsoon Rainfall
During the monsoon season (July to September), Punjab often receives excessive rainfall in a short period, causing rivers and drainage systems to overflow.
🌊 Overflowing Rivers
Punjab is crossed by major rivers like the Sutlej, Beas, and Ravi. Sudden heavy rains or excessive upstream water release can cause these rivers to overflow their banks, leading to floods. .
🏗️ Poor Drainage System
In many areas, especially rural regions, the drainage infrastructure is outdated or insufficient, preventing proper drainage of rainwater and leading to waterlogging and floods.
🏙️ Deforestation
Deforestation in the catchment areas reduces the ability of the soil to absorb rainwater, causing more surface runoff and increasing the risk of floods.
🛠️ Encroachment of Waterways
Illegal construction and encroachment on riverbeds and floodplains restrict natural flow paths of rivers, causing water to accumulate in populated areas.
🌩️ Climate Change
Climate change increases the frequency and intensity of extreme weather events, such as intense rainfall and cloudbursts, contributing to sudden flooding.
⛏️ Melting of Glaciers in Himalayas
Punjab receives water from rivers originating in the Himalayas. Rapid glacier melt due to rising temperatures increases river flow beyond normal capacity. increasing flood risk
🧱 Inadequate Reservoir Management
Improper management of dams and reservoirs upstream can lead to the sudden release of water during heavy rainfall, contributing to downstream flooding in Punjab.
4 / 8 • Impact

Who and what gets affected

🚶‍♂️ Displacement
Families are forced to leave their homes, losing shelter and security, often depending on temporary camps.
🌾 Crop loss
Fields get waterlogged, soil covered with silt, reducing harvest and farmers’ income, while replanting adds more costs.
🏚️ Home damage
Floodwater damages walls, floors, wiring, and appliances. Houses may become unsafe for living until repaired.
🦟 Disease
Standing water spreads diarrhea, skin infections, and mosquito-borne diseases like malaria and dengue.
🛣️ Roads
Connectivity breaks as bridges weaken and low-lying roads get submerged, delaying supplies and transport..
🚰 Water unsafe
Drinking water sources get contaminated, making people dependent on boiled or treated water.
🐄 Livestock
Animals face infections, hunger, and stress. Many may die if fodder, shelter, or veterinary help is lacking.
🧠 Stress
People suffer fear, anxiety, and fatigue. The loss of home, income, or loved ones worsens mental health.
🛶 Relief
Boats, shelters, and food aid are needed. Communities depend on outside help for survival and recovery.

6 / 8 • Safety

Flood management steps

⬆️ Go higher
Stay out of basements and low floors. Move quickly to the roof or upper levels. Identify a safe shelter beforehand.
⚡ Power/Gas off
Turn off electricity and gas if safe. Wet wires cause shocks or fire. Reconnect only after safety checks.
🎒 Grab kit
Keep a ready bag with water, food, medicines, torch, documents, cash, and power bank near exit.
🚫 Avoid water
Hidden Don’t walk in floodwater. It hides drains, currents, or electric wires. Even shallow water can be risky.
🚗 Don't drive in
30 cm water can float a car. Always take higher roads and follow barricades or diversion signs.
🧼 Safe water
Tap water may be unsafe. Boil for 3 minutes or use chlorine tablets. Keep tanks disinfected regularly.
🧒 Support vulnerable
Help kids, elderly, and disabled. Evacuate them early with required medicines and identity proofs.
🪢 Use ropes
Use ropes for balance and support. Avoid hand holding. Secure the rope tightly to a strong point.
🦺 After flood
Inspect your house carefully. Avoid live wires and broken poles. Clean and disinfect everything touched by water.
Evacuate early if: release warning, water rising to ground floor, or heavy night rain forecast.
7 / 8 • Kit

Emergency kit — pack for 3 days (household)

🎒 Essentials
  • Water (1–2 L/person/day), ORS
  • Dry food; baby/pet supplies (as needed)
  • Meds + prescriptions; first‑aid (bandages, antiseptic)
  • Torch + spare batteries; whistle
  • Phone + power bank + cables
  • Battery/hand‑crank radio
🗂️ Documents
  • IDs (Aadhaar, PAN), bank cards/passbook
  • Land/house papers; insurance policies
  • Printed contacts & local map
  • Waterproof pouches; cloud scans & a USB copy
🧰 Tools & hygiene
  • Rope, tape, multi‑tool, gloves
  • Soap, sanitizer, sanitary items
  • Masks, sturdy shoes, rain protection
  • Candles/matches or lighter
  • Cash (small notes)
Keep kit near an exit. Update seasonally. Park vehicles on higher ground before heavy rain.
